# Day 1 - CRM Basics

## 1. What is CRM
CRM (Customer Relationship Management) is a system that helps businesses manage relationships with customers and prospects. It centralizes customer data, tracks interactions, and streamlines processes to improve sales, service, and overall customer satisfaction.

## 2. Why Companies Use Salesforce
Salesforce is the world’s leading CRM platform. Companies use it to:
- Organize customer and prospect information in one place.
- Automate sales and service workflows.
- Track leads, opportunities, and deals.
- Improve collaboration across teams.
- Deliver personalized customer experiences.

## 3. Key Concepts
- **Account** → Represents a company or organization.
- **Contact** → A person associated with that company (employee, decision-maker, etc.).
- **Opportunity** → A potential deal or sale linked to an account and contact.

## 4. Business Flow
**Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer**

- **Lead**: A raw prospect (someone who shows interest).
- **Contact**: Once qualified, the lead becomes a contact (a real person tied to an account).
- **Opportunity**: A potential deal is created with that contact/account.
- **Customer**: When the opportunity is closed successfully, the account becomes a paying customer.

## 5. Real-World Mapping (Hospital System Example)
- **Account = Hospital** (the organization providing healthcare)
- **Contact = Patient** (the individual receiving care)
- **Lead = New Inquiry** (someone who calls or registers interest in treatment)
- **Opportunity = Treatment Plan/Admission** (the potential service or procedure offered)
